Step 3

Reinforced Table

By strengthening the posts with supports, you will give the structure more ability to resist loads. In this build, you will make a bigger, lightweight structure that has relatively flimsy legs. With each additional step you will make the legs stronger and eventually be able to hold up a ten pound weight!

Place the Base Plate with the grooves facing up. This table will be built upside-down, and turned upright when it's being tested.

Attach the Girder 120's to the base plate in the same slots shown here. Then place the Girder 60's on top. Make sure that the corners of the Angle Girders are all facing the correct direction, or the next steps will not work.

Attach the Angle Girder 15's. They are each three spaces away from every Angle Girder 120.

STOP

Flip the table over, and test its ability to support a 10lb weight. Watch out for your fingers! This structure isn't very sturdy - YET! continue to the next step.

Use the Connecting Strip 15 and two Rivet 4's while attaching struts to one another.

Attach I-Strut 120's to I-Strut 75's, and I-Strut 45's to I-Strut 75's. Then attach the combined struts to the 7th bore of the Angle Girder 120's

STOP

Flip the table over, and test its ability to support a 10lb weight. It's better than before, but it's not done yet.

Attach the X-Strut 63.6's to the Angle Girder 15's, then the Angle Girder 120's.

Great job! the finished project should look like this...

Now the table is finished! The final step of adding X-Struts at angles creates a triangle shape at both sides of each leg. Triangles are the strongest geometric shape, and are used to reinforce objects without adding excessive amounts of material.
